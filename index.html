Why GhostConvert?

Traditional online converters force you to upload sensitive files to their servers — creating risks of interception, logging, and unauthorized scanning.

GhostConvert solves this by leveraging WebAssembly and modern browser APIs. Every conversion runs entirely within your browser's sandbox.

This means: when you drop a file, the entire pipeline—reading, decoding, transforming, and re-encoding—runs locally on your CPU and memory. No network traffic is ever consumed.

Beginner's Guide: How to Convert Files Locally

  1. Prepare your files: Choose any number of images (including HEIC, JPG, PNG), videos, or spreadsheets. You can even select an entire folder.
  2. Import securely: Drag files into the dashed area or click to browse. The browser creates a local read-only stream—no server upload ever occurs.
  3. Pick an output format: Select JPG, PNG, WebP, PDF, or MP3 from the buttons above.
  4. Start encoding: Hit the convert button. A progress indicator shows each file's status. Speed depends on your local hardware.
  5. Save locally: Once done, click the generated files to download them straight to your default download folder.

Supported Formats

  1. Image Input: HEIC, JPG, JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF, BMP, AVIF, SVG, TIFF, TIF
  2. Image Output: JPG (Standard, universal compatibility), PNG (High quality, supports transparency), WebP (Very small size, ideal for web)
  3. Document Input: DOCX, XLSX, PPTX, CSV, TXT, ODT, ODS, EPUB, HTML, HTM. Note: WPS/ET/DPS please save as DOCX/XLSX/PPTX first. Old DOC/PPT formats are not supported directly, please save as the new format.
  4. PDF Input/Output: Merge images into PDF, extract PDF pages to images (JPG/PNG/WebP).
  5. Audio Input: MP3, WAV, OGG, FLAC, M4A, AAC, WMA
  6. Video Input (Audio Extraction): MP4, MOV, MKV, WebM, M4V, AVI, FLV, 3GP, MPEG, TS, MTS, M2TS, VOB, OGV, DivX, Xvid
  7. Audio Output: MP3 (128kbps universal format), falls back to WAV if encoding fails.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are my files really not uploaded?

Absolutely. Disconnect the network after loading the page and try—everything still works.

Why do large files sometimes fail?

Browser memory limits (~1.5 GB per tab) can stall huge files. For very large conversions, consider a dedicated desktop app.

Why does formatting get lost in Word/Excel?

Our lightweight, offline-friendly parsers don't support every advanced layout feature yet. We're continuously improving compatibility.
